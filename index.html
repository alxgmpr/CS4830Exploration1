Previously

My affinity for web development started by picking up HTML and CSS books at the library. At that point, viewing a web page's source was enough for me. But this taste grew as I got older and I found myself intrigued with how our Internet is built upon.

This meant I was playing with scripting languages whenever possible. I installed a LAMP server on my depressingly antiquated Gateway tower so that I could simulate client-server interaction.

As I got older I started to build more feature filled websites. I gained experience with CMS' like Wordpress and Joomla, enjoying playing with their source code. These CMS are what introduced me to PHP and server-side programming.

Today, I'm experienced in not only web scripting languages, but object-oriented ones as well, such as Java, C, and Python. I still deeply love web development, and now enjoy working with PHP and MySQL for personal projects.

When I was 18, I founded my own company, Fused Claptons LLC, in my dorm room at Mizzou (freshman year). I specialized in building incredibly intricate heating elements for e-cigarette vaporizers.

The now-defunct company saw good growth over it's 6 active months. I developed recurring wholesale relationships with vendors overseas while also handling product manufacture, packaging, and shipping distribution from my bedroom.

I also built the e-commerce backend with Wordpress and Shopify, gaining valuable experience not only by uniting the two, but also with credit card merchant processing and payment handling.

For me, this project was great because I culminated almost all of my web design knowledge into something that had more purpose than dummy LAMP servers on my old desktop. I even designed the logo myself using my web forum knowledge and the Adobe Creative Suite.

Currently

Although my company is no longer active or functional, I occupy my time studying computer science whilst buying, selling, and collecting rare sneakers and clothes. To most it sounds understandably odd, but the hobby is a large part of me now.

My Sophomore year of high school I gained an interest in the skateshop and streetwear brand Supreme. From that interest I made a habit of buying and selling the brand's rare and valued hats. These were bought and sold mainly through eBay, which gave me valuable experience that I use today.

Today, my personal programming projects include applications designed to help me acquire popular sneakers quickly and at low costs. These (unfinished!) projects and tools are available on my Github.
